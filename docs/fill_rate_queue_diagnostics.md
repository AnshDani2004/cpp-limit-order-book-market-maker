# Fill-Rate And Queue Diagnostics

This note documents the public technical extension for market maker fill-rate mechanics. It is a diagnostic exercise, not a profitability claim.

The artifacts are generated by:

```bash
python3 scripts/generate_fill_rate_diagnostics.py
```

Default run scope:

```text
events per run 5000
seeds 2
regimes low volatility, high volatility, trending
strategies naive symmetric, avellaneda stoikov
physical scenarios 13
zero_queue_control derived from quote rows with zero displayed queue ahead
```

The run is intentionally smaller than Stage 5C. It is meant to explain mechanisms cheaply and reproducibly, not to replace the 30-seed statistical pass.

## Artifact Set

```text
artifacts/fill_diagnostics/quote_lifecycle.csv
artifacts/fill_diagnostics/execution_opportunities.csv
artifacts/fill_diagnostics/fill_decomposition_by_seed.csv
artifacts/fill_diagnostics/fill_decomposition_summary.csv
artifacts/fill_diagnostics/strategy_comparison_by_seed.csv
artifacts/fill_diagnostics/strategy_comparison_summary.csv
artifacts/fill_diagnostics/statistical_summary.csv
artifacts/fill_diagnostics/paired_differences.csv
artifacts/fill_diagnostics/run_config.csv
```

`quote_lifecycle.csv` records every market maker quote submitted by the diagnostic runs. It includes quote ID, strategy, seed, regime, flow type, side, price, size, submitted event index, first fill event if any, lifetime in events, initial displayed queue ahead, displayed depth at price, filled size, remaining size, final status, and no-fill reason.

`execution_opportunities.csv` records the book state immediately before each synthetic external market event. It captures the aggressive side, touched price, best bid/ask before the event, displayed depth at the touched level, whether a market maker quote was at that level, queue quantity ahead of the market maker, size executed before reaching the market maker, market maker fill size from the event, and the no-fill reason.

## Taxonomy

The deterministic quote outcome taxonomy is:

```text
filled
partially_filled
canceled_before_execution
replaced_before_execution
never_touched_by_opposite_flow
price_not_at_best
insufficient_opposite_execution_volume
behind_queue
inventory_limit_blocked_quote
quote_size_too_large_for_available_flow
spread_too_wide
crossed_or_invalid_quote_rejected
terminal_liquidation_only
unknown_or_unclassified
```

The current generator uses the subset that can be measured directly from the simulator outputs: filled, partially_filled, canceled_before_execution, replaced_before_execution, never_touched_by_opposite_flow, behind_queue, spread_too_wide, insufficient_opposite_execution_volume, quote_not_at_touched_price, and no_opposite_liquidity. Categories that require a rejected quote or terminal-only taker path remain reserved and documented so future diagnostics do not invent incompatible labels.

## Main Result

Under the default diagnostic run, hand-chosen flow produces high maker fill probability, while ITCH-calibrated flow produces sparse maker fills:

| Scenario | Strategy | High Vol | Low Vol | Trending |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| hand_chosen_flow | naive | 0.5285 | 0.7510 | 0.5320 |
| hand_chosen_flow | Avellaneda Stoikov | 0.5315 | 0.7510 | 0.5345 |
| itch_calibrated_flow | naive | 0.0195 | 0.0210 | 0.0220 |
| itch_calibrated_flow | Avellaneda Stoikov | 0.0195 | 0.0210 | 0.0220 |

This repeats the Stage 5B and Stage 5D mechanism in a more explicit artifact set. The collapse is not primarily a strategy-choice result. Naive and Avellaneda Stoikov have nearly identical fill rates under ITCH-calibrated flow in this bounded diagnostic.

## Sparse Executions Versus Queue Burial

The execution-opportunity audit points to sparse external executions as the dominant mechanism in the ITCH-calibrated runs.

For naive quotes:

| Scenario | High Vol executions | Low Vol executions | Trending executions |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| hand_chosen_flow | 1231.0 | 1293.0 | 1254.0 |
| itch_calibrated_flow | 24.5 | 24.0 | 23.0 |
| increased_execution_intensity_10x | 224.5 | 222.5 | 202.5 |

Queue misses are small in the ITCH-calibrated baseline:

```text
itch_calibrated_flow maker_missed_due_to_queue_events:
high volatility 0.0
low volatility 0.0
trending 0.0
```

The zero-queue control is a derived subset of quote lifecycles with zero initial displayed queue ahead. It is not a separate exchange simulation. Under ITCH-calibrated flow, this subset raises fill probability only to roughly `0.053` to `0.055` in the populated naive rows. Under hand-chosen flow, zero-queue fill probability remains roughly `0.70` to `0.75`. A quote at the front of the FIFO queue still needs an opposite-side execution to arrive.

## Execution Intensity Sweep

Increasing only the synthetic external market intensity under ITCH-calibrated flow moves fill rates in the expected direction:

| Scenario | Naive High Vol | Naive Low Vol | Naive Trending |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| itch_calibrated_flow | 0.0195 | 0.0210 | 0.0220 |
| increased_execution_intensity_2x | 0.0400 | 0.0435 | 0.0410 |
| increased_execution_intensity_5x | 0.0975 | 0.1010 | 0.0915 |
| increased_execution_intensity_10x | 0.1890 | 0.1970 | 0.1850 |

This is the cleanest diagnostic in the extension. It changes the binding external-flow mechanism directly and produces a monotone fill-rate response.

## Quote Size

Changing quote size under ITCH-calibrated flow changes PnL and inventory exposure, but not quote-count fill probability:

| Scenario | Naive High Vol | Naive Low Vol | Naive Trending |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| quote_size_1 | 0.0195 | 0.0210 | 0.0220 |
| quote_size_10 | 0.0195 | 0.0210 | 0.0220 |
| quote_size_100 | 0.0195 | 0.0210 | 0.0220 |

This means quote size is not the primary explanation for whether a quote gets touched in this simulator. It still matters economically once a fill occurs because larger quote size scales inventory and PnL.

## Quote Lifetime And Requote Frequency

Under ITCH-calibrated flow, quote replacement cadence has a visible mechanical effect:

| Scenario | Naive High Vol | Naive Low Vol | Naive Trending |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| requote_frequency_fast_5 | 0.0097 | 0.0105 | 0.0110 |
| itch_calibrated_flow, cadence 10 | 0.0195 | 0.0210 | 0.0220 |
| requote_frequency_slow_25 | 0.0475 | 0.0525 | 0.0550 |

Longer quote lifetimes give sparse executions more time to arrive. This is a simulator-design sensitivity, not a claim about optimal live requoting.

## Strategy Comparison

In this two-seed diagnostic, Avellaneda Stoikov does not show broad PnL or fill-rate dominance.

Under hand-chosen flow:

```text
low volatility mean net PnL: 34377.0550 naive, 30615.0550 AS
high volatility mean net PnL: -12466.1332 naive, -14755.5251 AS
trending mean net PnL: 104870.0044 naive, 102034.7880 AS
```

Under ITCH-calibrated flow:

```text
low volatility mean fill rate: 0.0210 naive, 0.0210 AS
high volatility mean fill rate: 0.0195 naive, 0.0195 AS
trending mean fill rate: 0.0220 naive, 0.0220 AS
```

The diagnostic therefore agrees with the Stage 5C narrowed interpretation: AS effects are regime and flow dependent. In sparse ITCH-calibrated flow, there are too few fills for a strong strategy comparison, and the fill-rate mechanism is dominated by external execution scarcity rather than quote placement sophistication.

## Robust Conclusions

1. ITCH-calibrated flow has far fewer external execution opportunities than hand-chosen flow.
2. Increasing external execution intensity raises fill rates monotonically.
3. Queue depth is a real filter when depth exists, but it is not the dominant cause of the ITCH-calibrated fill-rate collapse in these runs.
4. Quote size changes economic exposure after fills, but does not materially change quote-count fill probability in the checked size sweep.
5. Slower requoting raises fill probability in sparse flow by increasing quote lifetime.
6. Naive versus Avellaneda Stoikov fill-rate differences are small relative to the flow-profile effect.

## Simulation-Design Limits

These diagnostics are still synthetic. The ITCH-calibrated flow uses a bounded QQQ sample to set event mix and size distribution, but the simulator does not reconstruct a full exchange session, participant identities, hidden liquidity, queue priority from real order entry timestamps, or all venue-specific order attributes.

The zero-queue control is a subset analysis, not a separate exchange matching rule. The default diagnostic uses two seeds and should not be interpreted as a confidence-interval result. Stage 5C remains the stronger statistical pass for strategy comparisons; this extension is designed to explain fill-rate mechanics.
